The Importance of a Modern Educational System

When you hear the word “education”, what comes to your mind? I can tell you what comes to my mind- notes desperately scribbled while trying to keep up with the teacher, not even understanding the lesson; hours spent completing assignments; weeks spent worrying over tests or examinations. I am sure that the majority of you would think something similar . What about the word “science”? When I was younger, that word made me think of discovery, whether it be of a new type of star, a new species, or a new technology; now, when I hear the word “science”, I still think of that, but now I mostly think of tests and assignments. What about the word “astronomy”? When I hear that word, I think of this one quote by the astronomer and writer Carl Sagan:

“There is a place with four suns in the sky- red, white, blue, and yellow; two of them are so close that they touch, and star-stuff flows between them. I know of a world with a million moons. I know of a sun the size of Earth- and made of diamond. There are atomic nuclei a few miles across which rotate thirty times a second… There are turbulent plasmas writhing with X- and gamma-rays and mighty stellar explosions. There are, perhaps, places which are outside our universe. The universe is vast and awesome, and for the first time we are becoming a part of it.”...
